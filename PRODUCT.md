# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

ios

## Users

People in couples who are curious-leaning about non-monogamy (NM) and cautious about the partner step. "Excited" and "anxious" are two temperatures of the same person, not two populations. Anyone in the app is already curious and partner-cautious, not paralyzed (the truly anxious never installed it). Their hesitation is rarely about NM itself; it is about the partner invite, and about not yet knowing their own shape ("I want NM" is a direction, not a settled want).

The relationship happens off-app, in the real world. Vayl is used in quiet, private, often emotionally weighted moments: solo self-reflection, or side-by-side with a partner during a Card Session. There are two lanes of the same path. A **solo self-discovery bridge** with genuine standalone value (clarity in either direction), and the **dyadic core** (Desire Map, sessions) where the real value lives. The primary relationship is the couple; the solo user is the same person earlier on the path.

## Product Purpose

Vayl gives couples the maps, vocabulary, and mirrors to explore non-monogamy together, and lets them make the determinations themselves. It is a discovery tool, not a clinical or therapy tool, and never an assessment: it names what a user said, and never infers what they didn't. Core surfaces are the Desire Map (compare two people's answers for overlap and distance), Card Sessions (a protected, two-device, safe-worded shared experience), Pulse (a lightweight relational check-in), and Learn (research-grounded vocabulary and content).

Success is a couple reaching a durable, honest conclusion: a clear "yes, together" or an equally respected "not now / not for me." A confident either-way answer is a win; engagement is not the metric.

## Positioning

Vayl gives couples maps, vocabulary, and mirrors for exploring non-monogamy, and lets them make the determinations, never a finding about who they are. The bright line every screen reinforces: name what the user said, never hand down a verdict.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, gravitational, intimate. A quiet dark room, not a dashboard: deep-space / bioluminescent, with a single earned spectrum gradient and a slow, breathing motion register. Three words: **humble, honest, unhurried.** The voice addresses one person ("you"/"I"), never "we"/"our," and never infers a partner's mind. No em dashes in copy. It is a small, optional, respected corner of a couple's life, confident enough to offer a real off-ramp, because that honesty is what makes an eventual "yes" durable.

## Anti-references

- Engagement-maximizing consumer apps: streaks, push spam, "open to find out" hooks, self-important notifications (a breakup needs no in-app alert). Vayl assumes it is NOT the center of the user's world.
- Clinical / assessment / personality-quiz products that issue findings about a person ("You are an Explorer," "you're an introvert"). Vayl compares points or ranks a person's own answers; it never characterizes the person.
- Bright, busy, gamified wellness dashboards. The register is a calm instrument, not a metrics surface.
- Funnel-shaped growth mechanics that pressure the partner invite. Guide by clarifying, never by prompting; the invite must feel like the user's own conclusion.

## Design Principles

- **Humility.** Vayl is a small, optional corner of a life. Right-size every feature; build the minimum that earns a respected place, never features that assume Vayl is the primary channel.
- **Discovery, not assessment.** Name what the user said; never infer what they didn't. Only two operations on quiz data: compare two points, or rank/distribute one person's own answers. End every quiz with a door to content, never a conclusion.
- **Two temperatures, one path.** Excited and anxious are states of one curious person. Guide by clarifying, not prompting, and keep an honest off-ramp; "not now" is a respected outcome.
- **Don't reach to connect features.** Design each feature for its own job; no cross-feature affordances or tie-ins unless explicitly wanted.
- **Slow, breathing, gravitational feel.** When in doubt, slower and softer. Motion conveys presence and state, never decoration or urgency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Ambient motion is fully disabled under Reduce Motion and Low Power Mode (the loop is removed, not just slowed); reactive feedback and one-shot effects always play. Haptics follow a weight-maps-to-consequence scale (light = navigate, medium = commit, rigid = two-device seal, heavy = safe-word only, success = terminal). Body text must clear WCAG AA contrast on the near-black surfaces. Touch targets meet the 44x44 pt iOS minimum. V1 ships dark-only by mandate, with light-mode token values authored but unwired for cheap post-launch enablement.
