The actual difference

At 5 rows, the card in the right version reads as a large panel dominating the lower two-thirds of the screen — the glass surface was designed for shorter, denser content (a couple of Map stat rows, a single headline), not a 5-item list; here it just outlines a big empty-feeling rectangle around content that already reads fine on its own. Void-native (left) lets each row breathe individually against the atmosphere instead of being boxed in, and it's honestly the closer match to how SelectablePill already looks in its own previews — full-width rows directly on the background, no wrapper.

My read: void-native. The card made sense when pills were a compact single row (there was a real "small discrete moment" to contain); at 5 full rows the content is substantial enough to hold its own, and the card just adds visual weight without adding clarity.
