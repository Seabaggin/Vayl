Why the lock-in exists

A session asks two people to be honest and undefended. It only pays off if they actually arrive — present, and choosing it on purpose. The lock-in is the threshold that makes starting a deliberate, shared act.

  • Marks the boundary — the room feels different the moment you cross in.
  • Requires intention — the friction turns "kill time" into "let's actually do this."
  • Certifies presence — the hold-and-release can't be done alone; both are here before a card turns.

The ritual settles emotion; the lock-in settles attention.
