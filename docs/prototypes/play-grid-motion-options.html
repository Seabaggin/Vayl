Deck grid — making it feel alive

A grid doesn't have to just sit there. The "alive" feeling comes from how it transitions between states — a compact resting form that opens into a fuller one, or a tile that becomes the thing you tapped. Here are the three families that fit a deck library. Each maps to a real iOS pattern, so they're native and performant, not gimmicks.

↓ Tap each phone to play the motion. Tap again to reverse.

1 Spread

Decks rest as a fanned pile (collapsed). A tap or pull spreads them out into the full grid. The most literal "collapse / spread" — it feels like dealing a hand of cards.
Good for: a tactile, card-native reveal. Watch: needs a clear affordance so people know to open it; the pile must hint "there's more here."

2 Hero-collapse

The featured hero is large at rest. As you engage the library, the hero recedes and the grid rises to take over the screen. Continuous and scroll-driven — the room reorganizes around your attention.
Good for: one screen that flows from "featured" to "browse" with no mode switch. Watch: the hero must collapse to something still useful, not vanish.

3 Expand-in-place

The grid is calm and static, but tapping a case grows it in place into the full detail (the others part and dim), and tapping back shrinks it home. The tile literally becomes the screen you opened. This is iOS's native zoom transition.
Good for: a premium, "magical continuity" feel with a calm grid. Watch: the grid itself stays still, so aliveness lives in the open/close, not the resting state.
