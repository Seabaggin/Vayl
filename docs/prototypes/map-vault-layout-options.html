My read

All three keep the Desire Map as the meaningful anchor and open into real screens. They differ in what the dashboard glance gives you:

  • A · Anchor + tiles — shows the most state at a glance and has the clearest focal point. Safest strong option. Risk: reads a little like an app grid.
  • B · One door — calmest and most compact, most deferential to the Pulse hero, and it feels intentional (a vault you open, not an exploded menu). Risk: hides state, so a glance tells you nothing specific until you tap in.
  • D · Lit ledger — directly answers your "a list feels underwhelming" by making the list a designed, lit object with real state peeks. Most distinctive, still compact. Risk: it lives or dies on the material execution; done flat it collapses back into a list.

My lean: A for the dashboard glance, or D if you want it to feel more like a crafted archive than a grid. B is the pick only if you want the Vault to almost disappear on the dashboard and live entirely as a destination.
