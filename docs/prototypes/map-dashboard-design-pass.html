Pulse · Me vs Us

Me = your one reading on the circumplex + your 30-logged arc, tap to check in or open the field.
Us = both of you on the same field (two auras), a plain-language "in sync / a step apart" read, no scoring, just the two points compared. Same aura language, one more dot.

Vault · what moved here

The record folds in from the old Me layer. The Vault is the kept pillar: Me holds your private map + journal; Us holds where you align, your agreements, and the record. Each shelf is a row that pushes into its own screen.

02The Path widget — what it does

The dashboard widget is not the trail. Its whole job in one glance: where are we, what's the next landmark, what structure are we on. It is a "you are here" ribbon, not a progress meter. Tap it and the full trail opens.

The collapsed widget shows four things, nothing more:
  • The active structure — a "◆ Swinging" chip (top-right). This is the couple's current path.
  • You are here — the current landmark ("The strip club") + its phase, in display type.
  • A mini-trail ribbon — done nodes bright, the now-node glowing, the road ahead dim + dotted. Territory behind and ahead, never a percentage.
  • Two seams — "3 of 13 · go at your own pace" (position, not %) and a quiet "Explore paths ›" (the paywalled door to other structures).

How it expands — tap the widget → the full trail opens as a protected cover → tap any node → its Mission Brief slides up.

03Explore other paths — one active, the rest a door

The widget stays about your journey: one active structure (Swinging, the default). "Explore paths" opens a gallery. Swinging is unlocked; the rest sit behind the couple unlock. Poly expands to its own variants (it is too vast for one trail), and a Custom builder lets a couple fork their own. Switching the active path is a deliberate confirm, never a casual toggle, because it re-frames the whole trail.

The mechanics I'm proposing (flag anything you'd cut):
  • Default, not a wall. Swinging is live from day one. New couples never hit an empty "choose your path" gate; they land already walking.
  • Preview before switch. Tapping a locked structure shows its phases + a couple of sample landmarks (read-only), then the unlock. You see what you'd get.
  • Switching is a confirm. "Make Poly · Kitchen-table your path?" keeps your record but re-frames the trail. Your walked landmarks that still apply stay walked.
  • Poly is a family, not a path. It expands to variants; a couple picks one to walk and can customize it, since poly is too broad for a single line.
  • Custom = a remix. The builder seeds from a preset, then you add / reorder / drop landmarks. This is where "pick and choose" lives.

04Open questions for you

Path widget height

Right now Pulse is the tallest (hero), Path is mid, Vault fills the rest. Does the Path ribbon feel like enough of a glance, or do you want it taller (more of the trail peeking)?

Me Path vs Us Path

For swinging they're nearly the same trail. Is the Me lens worth a distinct treatment (your readiness / your solo fork emphasized), or is Me Path just the same trail with "your course" framing?

Vault entry

Shelves-as-rows here. Alternative: one "Vault" block that opens to a shelf index. Rows feel more returnable; the single block is calmer. Which reads better to you?

Explore placement

"Explore paths" lives as a quiet seam in the widget footer. Should it instead live only inside the full trail (keep the widget dead simple), or stay on the dashboard?
