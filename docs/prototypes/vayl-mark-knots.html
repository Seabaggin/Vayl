The knot — a mark with rope in it

A knot is the most Vayl symbol there is: your own card says "the anchors that have kept you tethered to each other" and "Grounded · Secure." A knot is the tie that binds, two strands stronger bound than apart, and it's warm and tactile, not a cold glyph. The spectrum maps onto it directly: a cyan rope (you) and a magenta rope (them) tied through the purple where you hold. Every mark below is real twisted rope (layered stroke + dash + a roughen filter), not two crossed lines.

My recommendation

B — The Bond (reef knot). It's the one that's unmistakably two becoming one: a cyan rope and a magenta rope, equal, tied through each other at a purple center, with the tails left open (the relationship keeps going). It's the most legible at small sizes of the "two-rope" knots and it carries the cleanest meaning. A — Figure-8 (your pick) is the strongest single-rope story: one unbroken line, endlessly secure. I'd build B, and keep A as the alternate if you want one continuous thread over two.

Why it's Vayl

The first knot, the seed. The overhand is the simplest tie there is, the one inside every other knot. A loop with the rope tucked through: the smallest possible "we're tied." Quiet, foundational, intimate.

Scales: the loop+tuck is busy small; it wants to drop the tails and read as a single tucked loop under ~28px. Risk: simplest meaning, but also the least distinctive silhouette.
