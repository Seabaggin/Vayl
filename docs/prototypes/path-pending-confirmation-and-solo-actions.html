The Path — pending confirmation + solo actions

Two things the earlier full-flow mockup left undesigned on purpose: what a proposed-but-unconfirmed "already ours" actually looks like on the trail and inside the Mission Brief (§3.1, §9 item 1), and whether solo's version of the same management menu makes sense without a partner to confirm with. Both answered here.

01The pending node — a fifth state, visually related but never confusable

Pending borrows already-ours' teal (it's proposing the same claim) but stays hollow, dashed, and breathing at low opacity — never the solid filled diamond that means "confirmed." At a glance on a dense trail, a proposer or partner should never mistake "waiting" for "settled."

05The kebab menu — couple vs. solo

Same three-item shape, same position in the header, but solo's middle option can't mean "propose to a partner" because there's no partner. It follows the exact self-attestation pattern §5.1 already established for "I did this": solo's version is instant and unilateral, never a pending state, never a confirm/decline screen. Skip is reworded from "us" to "me" for the same reason.

Couple requires Alex
✎Edit this step
◇Mark already ours
⌀Skip — not for us

Tapping "Mark already ours" creates the pending node from section 01 — it needs Alex.

Solo just you
✎Edit this step
◆Mark true for me
⌀Skip — not for me

Same tap, filled diamond immediately — solo has no pending sub-state to pass through, exactly like solo's "I did this" needs no confirm step today.

What this resolves: the pending node is visually a hollow, dashed, low-opacity cousin of already-ours — related enough to read as "the same kind of claim," dimmed enough to never be mistaken for settled. The proposer's NodeView has nothing to press twice; the confirming partner's has a real yes/not-yet choice worded as gently as the rest of the trail. And solo's management menu now has an explicit, unilateral answer instead of silently inheriting a couple-only confirmation rule that never made sense for one person.
