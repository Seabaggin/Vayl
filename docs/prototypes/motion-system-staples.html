STAPLE 2

Weighted Arrival

Anything that enters as an object — a sheet, a cover, a card — arrives with mass: instant acceleration, sharp deceleration into rest, one confident settle, zero bounce. The curve is the deal physics curve timingCurve(0, 0, 0.2, 1). Sheets keep native interactive dismiss; this only styles the arrival. A cover is the same move, heavier: it carries you into the table world, so the register shift is the point.

STAPLE 3

Charged Tap

The one register that never changes. Press: the element takes weight (scale 0.96, hairline retracts) and two spectrum arcs sweep from top-center to meet at bottom-center — the circuit charges. Release: the circuit completes, glow bursts, haptic lands, action fires. Identical on a pill in Settings and a card in the OB. Press and hold the pill.

LAWS

Sequencing

LAW 3

Commit vs Cancel Dismissal

A sheet's exit inherits the intent that ended it. Commit: press and hold the Save pill, release — the circuit completes, the glow bursts, and the sheet's exit launches off the glow's decay. Cancel: no charge ever built, the sheet just leaves on the plain exit curve. Use the lag dial to find the handoff window: too long reads laggy, too short buries the glow.

The contract: every transition in Vayl is one of these three moves. Depth Handoff owns screen-level change, Weighted Arrival owns objects entering, Charged Tap owns input. Registers cap amplitude in tokens (quiet tier: scale delta ≤ 2%, travel ≤ 16pt) — ceremony-class motion (multi-second builds, dealer motifs) is banned outside OB and covers. Reduce Motion: staples 1–2 collapse to opacity; staple 3 skips arcs/glow, keeps the state change + haptic.
