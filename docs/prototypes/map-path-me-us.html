The Path — Me vs Us

Symmetry held: the same trail on both lenses. What changes is the annotation layer. Us is the road itself, the shared trail and where you are on it together. Me is that same road, privately annotated by you: a self-set stance on each step ahead, and a private note pinned to any landmark. Everything on the Me layer is authored by you and stays yours until you choose to share. No scores, no meter.

Us · the road

The shared trail. Joint position, the walked landmarks, and the Mission Brief on each node. One road, both of you.

Me · your read on the road

The identical trail, with your private stance per upcoming step and your private note on any landmark. A transparency laid over the one road, not a second road.

01Your private stances

Four self-chosen flags, only on the Me lens. You name where you stand. The app never computes it, never scores it, and your partner never sees it unless you share it. This is the anxious "am I ready for this next step?" given a private, pressure-free voice.

Why this stays honest (the guardrails from the debate): the Me layer only ever names what you chose (a stance you set, a note you wrote), never a computed readiness score, so it stays a mirror, not an assessment.
