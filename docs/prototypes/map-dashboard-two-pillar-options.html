A · Two Heroes

Symmetric weight

Honest about the new feature set: with only two pillars, both deserve hero treatment. Reuses Pulse's exact glow/scale language for the Vault, so the screen doesn't read as "one big thing and one small thing bolted on."

Risk: two big glowing spheres back to back may compete for the same kind of attention — needs a clear visual cue (colour, shape) that these are different kinds of things, not two Pulses.

B · Glance + Door

Information first

Surfaces the two new launch features (Agreements, Journal) as real, glanceable content — a proposed agreement or a recent entry is visible without opening anything, which makes Vault feel alive rather than a locked box you have no reason to check.

Risk: most complex option — a glance card + a separate door is two components to build and keep in sync, and the glance needs real empty-state handling for new couples.

C · Continuous Flow

Editorial, calm

Least chrome, most "returnable home" feeling — no card borders competing with the Pulse hero, just one soft "Together" rule and plain rows. Cheapest to build (no new card component, mostly typography).

Risk: plain rows may under-sell agreements/journal as "features" — closest to option A's opposite: everything reads as equally minor, when agreements are meant to feel important.
