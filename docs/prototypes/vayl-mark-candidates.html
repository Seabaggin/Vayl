Candidate C — The Convergence. The name made visible. "Vayl" is heard as veil; the product's whole act is lifting it. This is a diamond aperture mid-open, the top vertex parted like a seam, a point of light behind it. You're looking through the veil to the thing revealed.

Why it's Vayl

The spectrum becomes the story. Two threads rise on the 45° lattice grain, cyan (you) and magenta (them), and converge into a single luminous purple node, the bridge color, exactly where you meet. The brand's gradient stops aren't decoration here; they're the two people and their overlap.

Scales: at 20px it simplifies to two straight strokes meeting at a bright node, still legible and still "two-into-one". Risk: it has an up/down orientation (the others are radially symmetric), so it must always sit upright.

Why it's Vayl

The initial, instantly. A bold V is the fastest possible brand read, and the V is already the lattice's diagonal, so it's on-grain. Cut as a faceted chevron (an outer stroke + an inner offset thread) it reads as a gem / a funnel narrowing to a point.

It's also a downward aperture, a thing that channels two sides to one base, which rhymes with the convergence idea but as a letter.

Scales: the best brand-recognition at any size (it's a letter). Risk: a single letterform is the least conceptual and can feel like a monogram rather than a mark with meaning; closest to "just the wordmark's V".
