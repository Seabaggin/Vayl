The aura, as light not a ball

Same atmospheric space, four ways to render the presence so it reads as luminous and ethereal (Vayl) instead of a solid sphere. The fixes versus the ball: no glossy highlight, no hard edge.

1 · Soft star
the atmosphere
An edgeless bloom of light. Centered glow, fades to nothing. Calmest, most ethereal.
2 · Spectrum ring
the card-face
A 1D glowing contour, cyan→purple→magenta, two-pass glow. Strictest match to your "outline, no fill" rule.
3 · Nebula
living weather
A soft multi-hue cloud, slow drift, no edge. Most alive, reads as a state's "weather."
4 · Constellation star
the constellation
A bright core with soft rays and a few drifting particles. Ties to your reveal language.

My lean: 3 (Nebula) or 1 (Soft star), both read as a luminous presence rather than an object, and both scale down cleanly to the small auras in the field and the comparison thread. 2 (Spectrum ring) is the most literally on-brand with the card faces but reads a touch more like a node/portal than a felt state. 4 (Constellation) is the most distinctive but busiest, and two of them plus a thread could get noisy in the Us view. Tell me which form feels right and I will re-render the field, the comparison, and the Home widget with it.
