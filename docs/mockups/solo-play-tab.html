Insight · Desire
The instinct to figure out how to say something before saying it often isn't about clarity — it's about pre-emptive protection. Getting the words perfect is a way of controlling the response before it happens.

Research on desire disclosure consistently finds that starting with duration — "I've been sitting with this for a while" — reduces defensive responses significantly more than leading with the want itself.
— Gottman Institute · longitudinal relationship research
